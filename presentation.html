How the AI Assistant Works

A plain-English guide demonstrating how our system automatically handles Facebook browser scanning, scores safety compliance, and calibrates itself to your style.

Step 01

Browser Automation Layer

Before AI can analyze any content, the system must securely retrieve it. The Browser Automation layer operates as the hands and eyes of the system:

  • Group Navigation: Automatically logs in and navigates directly to your Facebook Group's moderation and admin queues.
  • Data Extraction: Pulls post texts, photos, external hyperlinks, profile details (account age, warnings), and entry application answers.
  • Human Emulation: Operates with natural delays and scroll intervals to mimic human usage, protecting group accounts from platform blocks.
Step 02

AI Screening & Verification

Once retrieved by the browser layer, the AI inspects the text, links, and member profiles:

  • Spam Vocabulary: Scans text for click-bait phrases, money-making claims, or Telegram/WhatsApp funnel tags.
  • Links & Media Scan: Inspects links to block shortened redirects, fake giveaways, and phishing portals.
  • Guidelines Compliance: Cross-references post topics with your active rules (e.g. banning self-promotion or cryptocurrency ads).
Step 03

Evaluate & Rank Risk

Rather than guessing, the AI parses inputs and compiles mathematical ratings between 0% and 100%:

  • Risk Assessment: High risk weights trigger on new accounts or members with warnings history.
  • Confidence Score: Shows the AI's certainty. Clear spam or helpful posts yield high confidence ratings.
  • Human Queue Action: Sorts posts from highest-risk first, recommending Approve, Reject, or manual Review.

Step 04

The Smart Learning Loop

The system adapts to your unique group's style in real-time. When a moderator overrides the AI's recommendation, it calibrates itself:

  • Vocabulary Adjustments: Decrements trigger word weights when you approve misclassified items.
  • Reputation Weighting: Bumps author trust factor on manual approval, making future reviews faster.
  • Zero Loss Retention: Keeps detailed log histories to trace override trends and save moderator time.
